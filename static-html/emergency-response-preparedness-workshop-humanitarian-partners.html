Emergency Response Preparedness workshop for humanitarian partners

On 6-7 October the United Nations office coordinating humanitarian aid in the region together with UNICEF organized a training on emergency response preparedness for regional UN and non-UN humanitarian partners in Istanbul, Turkey. 

Facilitators equipped participants with practical understanding on how to develop, implement and monitor Minimum and Advanced Preparedness Actions, Contingency Plans, as well as identify and address preparedness gaps.

The participants took the training to actively promote the ERP roll-out in Caucasus and Central Asia countries both through advocacy and guidance to their country teams and co-facilitation of future workshops.

INFORM Global team members presented the recently released Index for Risk Management (INFORM), a tool created to identify countries or areas at risk of humanitarian emergencies. Based on INFORM data and local capacities, participants collectively agreed that Georgia, Kyrgyzstan and Tajikistan should be given the highest priority for boosting emergency response preparedness in 2016.

Overall, participants stressed the importance to continue the collective work to enhance emergency response capacity and preparedness level throughout the entire region.

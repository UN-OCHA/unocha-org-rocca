Enhancing emergency preparedness in Azerbaijan

The April 2016 escalations along the line of contact of the Nagorno Karabakh conflict zone has demonstrated that the situation could deteriorate any time. Both national and international humanitarian stakeholders need to be ready at all time to respond to the potential humanitarian consequences of a significant deterioration of the conflict.

As a preparedness measure, OCHA’s Regional Office for Caucasus, Central Asia and Ukraine, jointly with UNICEF’s Regional Office for Eastern and Central Europe and CIS and the UN Resident Coordinator’s Office in Azerbaijan, facilitated an emergency response preparedness workshop in Baku on 23-24 June 2016.Over 30 representatives from government authorities, the Red Cross/Crescent Movementand UN agencies participated in the workshop. The participants provided recommendations on the division of roles and responsibilities and agreed on advanced preparedness actions for a potential escalation of the Nagorno-Karabakh conflict. ROCCA will continue working with the Government of Azerbaijan and humanitarian stakeholders to implement the recommended actions.

22 Oct 2014, Fergana Valley: Central Asia's Fergana Valley spreads across eastern Uzbekistan, Kyrgyzstan and Tajikistan. The area is seismically active and also suffers from recurrent seasonal disasters. Many of these disasters transcend borders, a factor that can quickly complicate relief and recovery efforts. Credit: OCHA/G. Ibragimova
23 Oct 2014, Khujand, Tajikistan: Kyrgyzstan and Tajikistan have long recognized the likelihood and complexities of a potential large-scale disaster affecting people on both sides. This recognition has translated into several agreements and protocols between Kyrgyzstan and Tajikistan describing the governmental relationship in the event of a trans-boundary emergency or disaster. Credit: OCHA/Z.Nurmukhambetova
23 Oct 2014, Khujand, Tajikistan: Humanitarian organizations and authorities teamed up on both sides of the Kyrgyz-Tajik border to run a first of its kind simultaneous simulation exercise that tested preparedness to deal with a medium-scale disaster affecting people across the border. Credit: OCHA/Z.Nurmukhambetova
23 Oct 2014, Khujand, Tajikistan: The simulation exercise involved table-top response planning, coordination with the Government and local response teams, and a needs assessment mission to "affected areas." It was an opportunity within each country to test coordination among all parts of the humanitarian community. Credit: OCHA/Z.Nurmukhambetova
23 Oct 2014, Khujand, Tajikistan: A government representative at the crisis command centre briefs humanitarian partners on the scale of the emergency, security situation and latest updates during cross-border simulation exercise. Credit: OCHA/Z.Nurmukhambetova
23 Oct 2014, Isfana, Kyrgyzstan: Reps from the Kyrgyzstan Red Crescent Society, local authorities and OCHA identify response priorities during the cross-border simulation exercise. Credit: KRCS/E.Bryabina
23 Oct 2014, Isfana, Kyrgyzstan: OCHA's National Disaster Response Advisor makes sure that the humanitarian community works side-by-side with the Government, complementing and boosting their response efforts. Credit: KRCS/E.Bryabina
23 Oct 2014, Khujand, Tajikistan: Humanitarian NGOs and the National Red Crescent Society prepped local community members to act as affected population during rapid needs assessment missions. This woman says she lost her only source of income - her small business - to the disaster. Credit:OCHA/Z.Nurmukhambetova
23 Oct 2014, Khujand, Tajikistan: The needs assessment mission meant humanitarian workers could test their training in emergency assessments in a "real" environment. Credit:OCHA/Z.Nurmukhambetova
24 Oct 2014, Khujand, Tajikistan: On the last day of the simulation exercise, the Tajik Red Crescent and the Committee of Emergency Situations ran a series of first aid and search and rescue demonstrations. This part of the exercise focused on what local communities and volunteers should do in an emergency. Credit: OCHA/Z.Nurmukhambetova
